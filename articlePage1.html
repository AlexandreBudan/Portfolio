Article 1: Setting my internship in context

First of all, it is important to put into context what happened during my internship and to explain why it happened that way.

Continue reading...

To beginning

Internship search

How did it start?

Initially, I looked for an internship in my field of interest. I was looking for an internship in back-end development and Tcp Innovation contacted me following one of my Linkedin posts, offering me tasks such as Php, Python and CMS use.

After a 3-hour face-to-face interview with them, I was finally told that I'd been accepted and could do a 2-month internship with them.

Next step


How did my internship go and why did I stop after a week?

This is simple.

The tasks that were initially planned did not respect what was initially planned and noted on the internship agreement. Nor did the company's environment allow trainees to develop and work in an appropriate manner.

Here's a list of some of the tasks I've been assigned, which I'll come back to in more detail in the article “A typical day at TCP”:

  • Assignments for marketing communication students
  • Day-long documentation production
  • Modification of the boss's workspace in his own words

How did it work out?


After a few days of carrying out tasks that I considered to be on the verge of slavery, I turned myself in to my fellow trainees who were with me.

In fact, there were 5 trainees and 2 employees, including the boss, so I was able to talk to them and ask them if this was normal and that maybe it was just the beginning, that it would get better.

They confided in me that it was the same for them and that they had been enduring it for several weeks too.

So I took the step of breaking the internship contract by invoking one of the articles of the law in the Education Code. By the end of the week, all the trainees, including me, had drawn up our termination notice.

To conclude


Afterwards, we all tried to get him to sign our severance agreement, which he of course refused to do. In particular, he blamed one of the trainees, saying that he had turned us all against him and would not sign the agreements.

I tried many times to contact him again, whether it was the boss or my internship supervisor, with no response from either of them.

So I decided to move on. I tried to find another internship, but in my situation it wasn't possible, and since I'd already found my work-study placement, I still passed my second year.
